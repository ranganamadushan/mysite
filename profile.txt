A comb-type interdigitated electrode is a key component of a flexible chemi-
resistive sensor that has the potential to revolutionize the field of sensing
materials through the development of next-generation flexible sensors.
However, the technologies for fabricating flexible interdigitated electrodes
are often complex, and costly, and require specialized instruments such as
dedicated inkjet printers for printing electronics. In this study, we seek to
assess the feasibility of using a regular office inkjet printer (Commercially
available EPSON L130 ink tank document printer) to produce cost-effective,
flexible conductive interdigitated electrodes by depositing a conductive metal
nanoparticle ink on a flexible polymer substrate.
